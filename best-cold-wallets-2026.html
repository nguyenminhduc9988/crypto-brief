Why You Need a Hardware Wallet

'Not your keys, not your coins' isn't just a slogan—it's a lesson learned by FTX, Mt. Gox, Celsius, and millions of individual victims who trusted centralized platforms. A hardware wallet stores your private keys offline on a dedicated device, making them immune to malware, phishing, and exchange hacks. If you hold more than $500 in crypto, a $50–150 hardware wallet is the cheapest insurance you'll ever buy.

Cold vs. Hot Wallets

The core trade-off in crypto storage is security versus convenience. Cold wallets keep keys offline and untouchable; hot wallets stay online for instant access but are exposed to hacks.

🔥 Hot Wallet
  • •Keys live online / on an app
  • •Exposed to malware & phishing
  • •Free & instant access
  • •Great for small daily amounts
  • •Vulnerable to exchange failure
❄️ Cold Wallet
  • •Keys stored offline on a device
  • •Immune to remote hacks
  • •Costs $69–249
  • •Best for long-term holdings
  • •Survives exchange collapses
