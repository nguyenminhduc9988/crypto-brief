Layer 2 Scaling Explained: How Ethereum Gets Faster and Cheaper

Published June 2026 · Reading time: ~6 min

The Ethereum Scalability Problem

Ethereum's base layer processes roughly 15 transactions per second with gas fees ranging from $1-50+ during congestion. Compare this to Visa's 65,000 TPS. This limited capacity made Ethereum impractical for everyday transactions—buying a $4 coffee could cost $10 in gas fees. Layer 2 solutions solve this by processing transactions off-chain and posting batches to Ethereum's mainnet, inheriting its security while dramatically improving speed and reducing costs.

How Layer 2s Actually Work

Optimistic Rollups (Arbitrum, Optimism, Base): Process transactions on a separate chain, periodically posting compressed data to Ethereum. 'Optimistic' because they assume transactions are valid unless challenged. If someone detects fraud, they submit proof and invalid transactions are reversed. ZK-Rollups (zkSync, StarkNet, Polygon zkEVM): Use zero-knowledge proofs to mathematically verify every transaction before posting to Ethereum. More secure and faster finality, but more complex to build on. Both types reduce fees by 10-100x while maintaining 2,000-4,000 TPS.

Top Layer 2 Networks in 2026

Arbitrum One: Highest TVL among L2s ($15B+), massive DeFi ecosystem, low fees ($0.01-0.10 per transaction). Base (Coinbase's L2): Fastest-growing L2, seamless Coinbase integration, growing dApp ecosystem. Optimism: Ethereum's official rollup partner (Optimism Collective), Superchain vision. zkSync Era: Leading ZK-rollup with EVM compatibility and ultra-low fees. Polygon zkEVM: Major brand with enterprise partnerships. Scroll: Emerging ZK-rollup with strong developer interest. For users: bridging takes 5-10 minutes, then transactions are nearly instant.

How to Use Layer 2 Networks

Getting started is simpler than most think. Step 1: Bridge funds from Ethereum mainnet to your chosen L2 using the official bridge (Arbitrum Bridge, Base Bridge, etc.). This costs a one-time L1 gas fee ($2-10). Step 2: Switch your wallet's network to the L2. Step 3: Use DeFi protocols, trade on DEXs, and interact with dApps on the L2. Gas fees will be 95-99% lower than mainnet. Step 4: When you want to move funds back, use the bridge in reverse. Security is guaranteed by Ethereum's base layer—your funds are as safe as on mainnet.
